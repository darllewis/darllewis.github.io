Courses

  • Introduction to Data Science: Undergraduate data science course focusing on elements of statistics, machine learning, and statistical computing from theoretical and applied perspectives. Spring 2017, Fall 2017, Spring 2018.

  • Quantitative Principles in Cell and Molecular Biology: Undergraduate quantitative biology lab focusing on central concepts and experiments in cellular, molecular, and developmental biology with an emphasis on underlying physical and engineering principles. Fall 2016.

  • Statistics for Social Science: Undergraduate methods course providing an introduction to probability as well as key topics in statistics and how they can be used in the public policy realm. Spring 2016.

  • Modern Genetics and Public Policy: Undergraduate course examining modern genetics' implications for public policy focusing on health, law, consumer products, and criminal justice. Fall 2015.

  • Advanced Tools for LaTeX: Undergraduate seminar introducing advanced techniques for preparing LaTeX documents in the natural and social sciences. Spring 2015.

  • Quantitative Analysis I: Graduate probability and statistics course designed to allow students to explore data creatively and to conduct straightforward statistical analyses. Fall 2014.

  • Formal Political Analysis I: Graduate course providing a rigorous introduction to choice theory, social choice theory, and non-cooperative game theory. Spring 2013.

  • American Electoral Politics and Party Strategy: Undergraduate course in American political behavior, with a focus on party politics. Fall 2010.
