My research applies formal and empirical techniques to study federalism and intergovernmental relations. I explore federalism and intergovernmental relations, with a particular focus on the United States of America. The United States has successfully maintained a federated system of dual sovereignty between the states and the national government for nearly 250 years. My research develops models to understand the mechanisms driving this success with an eye towards the current state of other nations, including several Commonwealth states and the European Union.

I earned a B.S. in Applied Physics and Political Science with a minor in Aerospace in 2011 and a M.A. in Politics from Princeton in 2013.  I have also spent time on Capitol Hill working in the offices of Congresswoman Heather Wilson and Senator Pete Domenici.  

Whenever I need a distraction, I like to find my way to the nearest disc golf course or beach for a few hours or catch up on the latest news coming out of NASA, and I have also spent many hours on stage in a wide range of theatrical productions including several world premiers.  If you are around Princeton during the Spring and Summer months, feel free to get in touch with me or my co-captains about joining the Politics Department's softball team, Leviathan.
