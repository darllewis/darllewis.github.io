My research focuses on the interaction of political actors across different levels of government, ie, federalism. In particular, I am interested in understanding the forces that drive political actors to change their support in favor of a centralized national government where they had previously opposed centralization, as in the case of the United States in the late 19th Century and, more recently, Europe.  

Substantively, I focus on the role of developing transportation networks across the United States throughout the 19th century to demonstrate the link between interstate economic development and legislative support for expanding national jurisdiction over policy.  I also have projects branching off from this which explore the legislative dynamics behind the resulting policy outcomes using a public choice framework.
